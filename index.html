TOPIC OF INTERST

Based on the initial data analysis, we have found Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens have the most registered dogs. However, the Bronx and Staten Island have significantly fewer registered dogs. By comparing the registered dog counts with population, we have found that although there are significantly fewer registered dogs in the Bronx, there are only 6% population differences compared to Manhattan (Bronx 1,471,160; Manhattan 1,664,727).

CONSTITUENCIES

“Simultaneously material and ideological, the dichotomies may be expressed in the following chart of transitions from the comfortable old hierarchical dominations to the scary new networks I have called the informatics of domination.“[Donna Haraway]
In light of Donna Haraway’s “informatics of domination” tracing access points of power (APP) in the age of technology, this informatics of domination focuses specifically on APP relevant to the gig economy.
The APP informatics represents, instead of a constellation of constituencies, a structured and situated assembly that molds a hyper-individualized subjectivity compatible with the new configuration of economy — the gig economy. The unfinished[ing] informatics diagram tries to adumbrate the material reality and its convergence with technological mediation and its social embeddedness in the gig regime, one that is: advertised as a “comforting sense of technical neutrality and progressive openness.” [Tarleton Gillespie] characterized by Valerio De Stefano as the “casualization of the workforce, informalization of the formal economy, and the so called ‘demutualization of risk.’”[Valerio De Stefano].The diagram:counters the discourses that justify exploitative practices, privilege technologies, shift risk to workers. unveils the praxis of profiting from the reconfiguration of economy maps the intersectional movement across political economic and technological constituents
