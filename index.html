TOPIC OF INTEREST

at the convergence of labor， media， and platform

The project look at the assembly of gig economy workers and their patches of livelihood that pale into indifference under the gig economy and highly velocitized society, through their precarious working condition, the affective labor, and the Orwellian labor coordination of algorithms.
This project focuses on food delivery workers in China whose presence makes possible the elision of geopolitical distance that is crystallized to power by the digital undercurrent of the urban environment and the flow of capital. The project is composed of counter-narrativese of the management literature boosting gig economy, roiling waves of the modernization promises that spouses and reifies the dominion of existing power structure.
The objective is to increase the saliency of  the “casualization of the workforce, informalization of the formal economy, and the so called ‘demutualization of risk’”[Valerio De Stefano] concurrent with the gig economy.

CONSTITUENCIES

“Simultaneously material and ideological, the dichotomies may be expressed in the following chart of transitions from the comfortable old hierarchical dominations to the scary new networks I have called the informatics of domination.“ [Donna Haraway]
In light of Donna Haraway’s “informatics of domination” tracing access points of power in the age of technology, this informatics of domination focuses specifically on APP relevant to the gig economy.
This informatics represents, instead of a constellation of constituencies, a structured and situated assembly that molds a hyper-individualized subjectivity compatible with the new configuration of economy — the gig economy. The unfinished[ing] informatics diagram tries to adumbrate the material reality and its convergence with technological mediation and its social embeddedness in the gig regime, one that is: advertised as a “comforting sense of technical neutrality and progressive openness.” [Tarleton Gillespie] characterized by Valerio De Stefano as the “casualization of the workforce, informalization of the formal economy, and the so called ‘demutualization of risk.’”[Valerio De Stefano].The diagram:counters the discourses that justify exploitative practices, privilege technologies, shift risk to workers. unveils the praxis of profiting from the reconfiguration of economy maps the intersectional movement across political economic and technological constituents

SITE OF INTEREST

The choice is because of a personal experience. I use to live in Haidian District, Beijing, during that for one of my undergrad project [a pastiche city documentary of Berlin Symphony of a City, and the Man with a Video Camera]. I was given a chance to observe the city. It was the year 2017, right in the middle of the fierce competition among several food delivery platforms. Haidian, packed with tech companies and universities, was one of the major fields of their competition.
To capture the rhythm of urban life, I was out on the street for 20 hours each day. Most often I saw food deliveries men (Xiaoge) either sitting near the back rack or rushing on their electric bicycles.
But does site really matter?

Labor order under "digital control"*——a study on the labor control of delivery riders. . . Manufacture improved the way labor was organized. On the one hand, the division of labor increases productivity and creates "partial workers"; on the other hand, the labor hierarchy divides workers into skilled and unskilled workers. The division of labor and the hierarchy jointly promote the improvement of production efficiency, but at the same time lead to the depreciation of the value of labor force . . . In "Uber: Algorithms Redefining Work," Alex Rosenblat reveals how Uber uses algorithms to control its users -- drivers. . . Behind all this, Rosenblatt points out, “Uber’s algorithms give the company tremendous control over how drivers work” (Rosenblatt, 2019: 187). . . "Returning to the hidden place of production and exposing the obscured labor process and production scenes to the researchers' vision is the essence of labor research" (Wen Xiang, Zhou Xiao, 2007: 29). . . Survey

A survey of 6196 samples conducted by 199it research team and Chinese Academy of Social Sciences shows the distribution regarding age, education level, recreational activity, monthly income, channel for seeking help, demand from the government, demand from the employers, confidence level…
The survey actualized and contextualized the discussion of the social and occupational precarity of gig workers, the absence of legislative protection of this new form of labor, and the structural inequity of social distribution.
